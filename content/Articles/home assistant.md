It has been a while since I took any time to post here.  

In that time I purchased not one but two Raspberry Pi 4 B computers (don't ask) and started to dabble in some home automation with [Home Assistant](https://www.home-assistant.io/ "Home Assistant homepage").  

I got very, very deep into virtualiztion only to come back out on the other side and decide that the cloud was not in fact all it was cracked up to be.  We had our second kid, we began to move past the global pandemic sweeping the nation and I wrote a lot of Python.

For those uninitiated with Home Assistant, it is an open source operating system that can use a simple device (like an RPi) to automate things in your home.  Simple things like lights, doorbell etc.  It is very DIY - like you need to have comfort in a commandline and the ability to flash an OS to an SD card.  

While it takes a modicum of technical ability, it is not rocket science and I do not think a person needs to be super computer literate to get into it (if you make a new Apple ID each time you get a device, maybe sit this one out.)  Ironically the most useful plugin we have found was the Roku Remote, which allowed us to turn our iPhones into Roku Remotes via the app.

There were some trials and tribulations overall, but I cannot believe how much I learned from starting to get into the HA project and how deep it can really go.  There are some great features like an easy cloud plugin (its $5/month) and MFA that make it feel a little more comfortable to host yourself.

I strongly encourage anyone using Google Home or the other big services, take control of your own data, go open source and move things back to your home.
